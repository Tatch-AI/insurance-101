01 / REVENUE

How Harper Makes Money

Harper generates revenue through exactly two channels. Understanding these is fundamental to every decision in sales, service, and renewals.

$
Commissions
Paid by the insurance carrier to Harper for selling the policy. Typically around 10% of the premium. Received roughly two months after the policy is sold on agency bill; received instantly from the down payment on direct bill.
%
Broker Fees
Charged by Harper directly to the customer at the beginning of the policy. This is immediate revenue. On agency bill policies, broker fees can be split into earned (collected upfront) and unearned (spread over 10 payments).
Earned vs Unearned Broker Fees
Earned broker fees are collected immediately — it's money in the door today. Unearned broker fees are spread across the 10 monthly payments. You can adjust the earned/unearned split to lower a customer's down payment. If you make the broker fee all unearned, it rolls into the 10 monthly payments instead of being collected upfront — so the customer's down payment is lower. But if they miss a payment and cancel, you lose that unearned broker fee because the policy lapses before it's collected.

Example: $1,000 Policy at 10% Commission

PREMIUM$1,000
COMMISSION$100 (10% of premium)
BROKER FEE$100 (charged to customer)
CUSTOMER PAYS$200 today ($100 down + $100 fee)
TOTAL REVENUE$200 ($100 fee today + $100 commission in ~2 months)

Intake
Gather all information from the customer. Ask about insurance requirements, landlord contracts, and additional insured needs. Speed matters — customers don't want to be on the phone for 30 minutes.
MID FUNNEL

The most critical bottleneck. Job is to get the quote — either through instant quote or the E&S market. 50% of leads are lost here due to slow callbacks and follow-up. This stage must be airtight.
SALES
Sales
Pitch the quote to the customer, handle adjustments, and close the deal. Instant quotes can be pitched same-day. E&S quotes take longer. Sales should always check subjectivities and insurance requirements before closing.
SERVICE
Service
Handle endorsements, certificates of insurance, and any policy changes. Service catches everything that went wrong upstream. If service is perfect, renewals follow. If not, the customer walks.
RENEWALS
Renewals
Where the real profit lives. After year one, the account cycles between service and renewals indefinitely. Losing a customer in year 1–2 means the acquisition cost was wasted. Ideal retention: 7–9 years.
Why Mid Funnel is the Biggest Risk
Harper does ~200 intakes per day. If 50% are lost in mid funnel because of slow callbacks and no follow-up, no amount of sales effort can make up for it. Every employee's time who touched that lead — intake, engineering, service — is also wasted. The mid funnel team must push instant quotes out same-day and always try the admitted market first before going to E&S.
03 / POLICY TYPES

Direct Bill vs Agency Bill

There are two types of policies Harper sells. Everything about the process — speed, cost, complexity — depends on which bucket the policy falls into.

Direct Bill (DB) — The Simple Path

Insured (Customer)
↓
Harper
↓
Insurance CarrierNeXT, Coterie, Progressive

The insured pays the insurance company directly — Harper acts as the agent but there's no wholesale broker layer in between. Policies are issued quickly, and coverage is stronger relative to the E&S market. There are no taxes or fees, though Harper can still charge broker fees separately. This is the ideal path.

Agency Bill (AB) — The E&S Path

Insured (Customer)
↓
Harper
↓
Wholesale BrokerRT Specialties, etc.
↓
Insurance CarrierKinsale, etc.

An extra layer — the wholesale broker — slows everything down. This is why quotes take longer, there are taxes and fees, and everything is more complicated. The broker has to make money too, which adds cost.

What is "Main Street America"?
Think of it as walking down a typical street: a restaurant, a UPS store, a Staples, a grocery store. These are simple, low-exposure businesses that standard carriers are happy to insure — they go direct bill. Anything more complicated — a bar, a group home, a security company — standard carriers don't want the risk, so it goes to the E&S market (Excess & Surplus Lines).
Admitted vs Non-Admitted Carriers
Direct bill carriers are typically admitted — meaning they are regulated by the state and follow standard forms. E&S carriers are typically non-admitted — they decide what they want to write, what exclusions to apply, and can be much more restrictive. However, some E&S carriers can also offer admitted options in certain situations. Always try the admitted market first before going to E&S.

How Harper Loses Money: Chargebacks

When a customer cancels mid-term, Harper has to return the unearned portion of the commission back to the carrier.

Chargeback Example

Revenue Accuracy Matters
Chargebacks are the primary way Harper loses revenue. This is why the accounting piece is critical — the team must track revenue accurately and account for potential chargebacks at all times. The carriers and brokers will always get their money, no matter what. If we don't get it right, we eat the loss.
Direct Bill Revenue Collection
Commission is taken instantly from the down payment. Broker fee is charged at purchase — immediate revenue. Simple, fast, and predictable.
Agency Bill Revenue Collection
Commission arrives roughly two months later from the carrier. Broker fee can be split earned/unearned. More complex and harder to track, but often larger policies.
05 / BIND PROCESS

The Bind Process (E&S)

Once the customer agrees to a quote, this is the step-by-step process to actually get the policy issued. For instant quotes, most of this is automatic. For E&S — it's all manual.

1
Quote Received from Broker
The wholesale broker (e.g., RT Specialties) sends back a quote from the carrier (e.g., Kinsale). The quote includes coverage details, premium, and a list of subjectivities — requirements that must be met to issue the policy.
2
Create Bind Documents
Includes the applications, finance agreement, and all required forms. The bind docs should be sent to the customer within 2 hours of the sale. RT Connector creates the binder automatically, but Harper still creates the separate finance agreement.
3
Customer Signs
The customer must sign both the binder documents and the finance agreement. Both signatures are required before moving forward.
4
Harper Signs
After the customer signs, Harper co-signs the documents to authorize the bind.
5
Send Request to Bind
Harper tells the broker: "We have the insured's money, all documents are signed — buy it." Two critical pieces: the effective date (when coverage starts) and all subjectivity documents must be collected.
6
Binder Confirmation
The insurance carrier confirms the customer officially has coverage. This is the green light.
7
COI + Finance Upload
Harper sends out the Certificate of Insurance and uploads the finance agreement so the premium finance company can fund the carrier and broker.
Instant Quote vs E&S Timeline
Instant quotes skip most of these steps — the policy is issued immediately, no bind documents needed, no subjectivities to worry about. That's why the mid funnel team always tries the admitted market first.

E&S policies realistically take 24–48 hours to issue even after the customer pays. The customer must be told this upfront so they're not surprised.
06 / SUBJECTIVITIES

Subjectivities & Compliance

Subjectivities are the requirements that a carrier demands before they'll issue a policy. Miss one, and the entire process restarts.

📄
Loss Runs
A document showing claims history for the past 2–5 years. If the customer has been in business 2 years and had insurance, they should have 2 years of loss runs. No claims = ideal. Claims found after sale = quote may change and the whole process restarts.
📋
State Licenses
Required for certain industries. The carrier needs proof the insured is licensed to operate in their state. Missing this can trigger a notice of cancellation — even after the policy is issued.
👤
Resumes
Owner/operator resumes showing relevant experience. If the customer doesn't have one, Harper can offer to build one with a 5-minute phone call — ask about school, work experience, achievements.
📑
NPDB & Other Docs
National Practitioner Data Bank reports (for medical), due diligence forms, state-specific surplus lines forms, and any other carrier-specific requirements listed on the quote.
When to Communicate Subjectivities
There are two schools of thought:

Old approach: Mention subjectivities after payment to avoid scaring the customer. This caused cancellations and angry customers who felt blindsided.

Current approach: Mention the main subjectivities during the pitch while breaking down the policy. Prep the customer so there are no surprises. This leads to better service outcomes, fewer cancellations, and happier renewals.

As a Series A company, Harper must be transparent from the beginning. Customers need to know what they'll need before they pay.
Surplus Lines Form
Whenever a policy goes through the E&S market, a surplus lines affidavit is required. This form proves that the agent tried the admitted market first, got denied by at least three standard carriers, and then went to E&S as a last resort. It's a legal requirement showing due diligence.
07 / MINIMUM EARNED PREMIUM

MEP — The 25% Rule

On agency bill / E&S policies, 25% of the premium is fully earned the moment the policy is issued. No refunds. No exceptions.

Agency Bill / E&S
25% minimum earned premium — non-refundable from day one. If the customer's policy is $10,000, then $2,500 is locked in the moment it's issued. Even if the customer only paid a 15% down payment ($1,500), the finance company loses 10% and sends the customer to collections. This is why getting it right the first time is critical.
Direct Bill
Easy cancellation — the customer might lose $10–20 in fees. Maybe a full refund if they cancel the same day. The process is simple and low-risk compared to agency bill.
Real Example: Security Guard Policy
A security guard company was sold a policy. The customer sent their insurance requirements (from a contract) an hour after everything was issued. The policy couldn't meet the requirements. The customer lost $500 with no possible refund due to the 25% MEP. She filed a complaint with the insurance commissioner. Harper had all call logs and did everything right — but the outcome still hurt. This is why sales must verify all requirements before the bind.
08 / CERTIFICATES OF INSURANCE

COIs & Additional Insured

A Certificate of Insurance (COI) is proof that the customer has insurance. It's sent to third parties — landlords, clients, contract partners — to prove coverage.

What's on a COI
The insured's name, coverage types, coverage limits, effective dates, and any additional insured or certificate holders. For example: "Aarfan LLC needs Anunni LLC listed as a certificate holder at 181 South Park."
Who Requests COIs
Landlords, general contractors, clients, government agencies — anyone who needs proof that a business is insured before letting them operate, sign a lease, or fulfill a contract.
Blanket vs Specifically Listed Additional Insured
These are two completely different things. A blanket additional insured provides general coverage. A specifically listed additional insured names a particular entity. If someone requests specifically listed and you put blanket — and send the wrong COI — that could be a lawsuit. Every COI the VAs create gets double-checked by someone onshore because this must be perfect.

09 / ERRORS & OMISSIONS

Why Accuracy is Everything

One wrong number on a document can expose Harper to a lawsuit. This is called Errors & Omissions (E&O) — and it's the scariest risk in insurance brokerage.

The Coverage Limit Nightmare
Scenario: A customer buys a policy with $1M / $2M coverage limits. They request $1M / $3M. A COI gets sent out showing $1M / $3M. The customer gives it to the state, gets approved, and operates for two months.

Then: A claim happens — a kid slips, falls, breaks a tooth. Parents sue. The claim exceeds $2M.

The problem: The COI showed $3M aggregate, but the actual policy only has $2M. The carrier says: "This is your mistake. We issued $1M/$2M." Any lawyer will argue the customer was under the impression they had $3M because of the COI Harper sent. Harper is now on the hook for the extra $1M.

This is why every single COI is double-checked. Every coverage limit must match the actual policy. No exceptions.
⚠
Wrong Coverages
If coverage on a COI doesn't match the actual policy, the entire sale may need to be unwound. Lead goes back to mid funnel, everything restarts, and the deal is invalidated.
🔍
Double-Check Everything
Every COI created by VAs offshore is reviewed by someone onshore. Even a blank COI with no coverage listed is a risk — it went out, and someone could rely on it.
📦
Validated Deals
Sales reps who "validate" deals that don't actually close properly lose their commission. If something is wrong, the deal gets refunded and unvalidated. Selling right the first time matters more than volume.
10 / SALES PRINCIPLES

How to Sell at Harper

Practical advice from the training session on building relationships, closing deals, and avoiding common pitfalls.

🤝
Build Relationships Fast
You have 3–5 minutes to pitch and build a relationship. The customer can't just be a number on the board. Find common ground quickly. The guy who started at $3K with one limo now has 350 cars and a $1M+ policy — and he left the agency the moment his trusted rep left. Relationships are everything.
📷
Respond Like Crazy
Every text message, every phone call, every email — respond the same day, including Saturdays and Sundays. Response time is the #1 differentiator between Harper and every other broker. Nobody else gives attention to the $500 policy customer. We do.
💰
Always Be Closing (ABC)
Your job all day long: email customers, pitch quotes, close deals. Check Big Brother for quotes, pitch them, follow up. If an E&S quote looks like it might fit instant quote — ask mid funnel to try. A cheaper instant quote beats a slow E&S one every time.
🚀
Prep the Customer
Tell them about subjectivities during the pitch. Tell them if additional insured may cost extra. Tell them E&S policies take 24–48 hours. If they're prepped, service goes well, and they renew. If they're blindsided, they cancel — and you lose commission.
Buyers Are Liars
Customers will underreport revenue to get a cheaper quote. A gas station owner said he sold 15,000 gallons/month — the minimum contract requires 40,000. He actually did 42,000. He went from 15K to 42K in one conversation.

You must verify. Ask for P&Ls. Check if the numbers make sense. If two brokers quote the same customer with different revenue numbers, carriers like USLI will flag it and reject the bind. Harper looks bad, the deal dies, and you've wasted everyone's time.

When you misrepresent information — even based on what the customer told you — Harper is liable because we presented it to the carrier. All calls are recorded. Be honest, verify everything, and never stuff a policy into a bucket it doesn't belong in just to get a cheaper quote.
The USLI Cross-Quote Problem
If two brokers submit different revenue numbers for the same customer to USLI, the carrier will flag both quotes. They'll reject the lower one and demand proof (P&L) before issuing either. This stops the entire bind process and can result in a lost deal. Always ask: "Would you be able to show me a P&L that confirms this revenue?" It protects everyone.
