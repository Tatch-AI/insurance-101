Routing Rule: Quick Markets First
Always exhaust quick-market carriers (Next, Coterie, Hiscox, Progressive, Thimble) before routing to E&S. Quick markets deliver instant or same-day quotes with better coverage and no surplus line taxes. E&S is the last resort — used only when the admitted market cannot accommodate the risk.

E&S Routing Rules

"Last Resort" E&S Warnings
  • 25% minimum earned premium — locked the moment coverage is issued. No refunds.
  • Annual audit risk — payroll or revenue misreporting results in massive adjustment bills.
  • Difficult endorsements — every change goes through the broker and takes days, not minutes.
  • Surplus lines affidavit required — legal proof that the admitted market was tried first (3+ declinations).
  • Customer must be told upfront: E&S policies take 24–48 hours to issue, even after payment.

02 / DIRECT CARRIERS

Direct Carriers

Direct carriers accept business directly from Harper with no wholesale broker intermediary. Policies are direct bill, coverage is admitted, and turnaround is fast.

What Makes a Carrier "Direct"
Harper has an appointed relationship with the carrier. Business is submitted through the carrier's portal or API, quotes are returned directly, and policies are issued without a broker layer. Commission is paid directly to Harper. This is the fastest, cleanest path for both the agency and the customer.

03 / MGAs & E&S MARKET

MGAs, Wholesale Brokers & E&S

When the admitted market cannot accommodate a risk, Harper routes through managing general agents (MGAs) and wholesale brokers to access the Excess & Surplus Lines market.

What Is an MGA?
A Managing General Agent is a specialized intermediary with underwriting authority from one or more carriers. MGAs can bind, issue, and manage policies on the carrier's behalf. They typically focus on niche industries or hard-to-place risks. Think of them as specialized arms of carriers.
What Is a Wholesale Broker?
A wholesale broker acts as a middleman between Harper (the retail agent) and the E&S carrier. They do not have binding authority — they submit on Harper's behalf and negotiate with carriers. They add a layer of cost and time, but provide access to markets Harper cannot reach directly.

E&S: Last Resort Reminders
  • Minimum Earned Premium (MEP): 25% of premium is non-refundable from day one. Customer pays even if they cancel the next day.
  • Audit Exposure: WC and GL audits are annual. Revenue or payroll misreporting triggers adjustment bills that the customer cannot avoid.
  • Endorsement Friction: Every policy change routes through the wholesale broker. Simple changes that take minutes on direct bill take days on E&S.
  • Surplus Lines Affidavit: Legal requirement proving the admitted market was tried first (3+ carrier declinations).
  • Customer Impact: Higher cost, slower service, more paperwork. Set expectations before the bind — not after.

Admitted vs Non-Admitted: Key Differences

Admitted Carriers
Regulated by the state insurance department. Use standardized policy forms (ISO forms). Rates are filed and approved by the state. If the carrier goes insolvent, the state guaranty fund covers claims. Most direct-bill carriers are admitted.

Always try admitted first.
Non-Admitted (E&S) Carriers
Not regulated by standard state forms. Carriers write their own terms, set their own exclusions, and price freely. No guaranty fund protection if the carrier fails. Surplus lines taxes always apply. Used when admitted carriers decline the risk.

Use only when admitted is unavailable.

State-Specific Nuances

Key State Considerations
  • Surplus lines taxes vary by state — some states add 3–5% to the premium, others more. Always factor this into quotes so the customer isn't surprised.
  • Some states require domestic surplus lines filings — the surplus lines affidavit must be filed with the state's surplus lines office, not just kept on file.
  • Workers' comp is state-regulated — rates and class codes differ by state. A class code in California may price differently than the same code in Texas.
  • Monopolistic WC states (Ohio, North Dakota, Washington, Wyoming) — employers must buy WC through the state fund. Private carriers cannot write WC in these states.
  • Carrier licensing varies — a carrier admitted in one state may not be admitted in another. Always confirm the carrier is licensed in the customer's state of operation.

Routing Quick-Reference

1 Identify the industry — Is this a "main street" business (restaurant, retail, professional services) or a specialty risk (security, bars, group homes)?
2 Match to carrier specialty — WC only? Try Employers or Guard. Daycare? Try Markel. Professional services? Try Hiscox. Small business <$5M? Start with Next.
3 Confirm state eligibility — Is the carrier admitted in the customer's state? Any state-specific requirements (monopolistic WC, filing obligations)?
4 Try the quick market — Run instant quotes through Next, Coterie, Hiscox, Progressive, or Thimble. If a quote comes back, pitch same-day.
5 E&S only if all else fails — Document the declinations. Submit through RT Specialty, RPS, or AmWINS. Set customer expectations on timeline and cost.
05 / SERVICE OPERATIONS

Service Operations

Service is where everything that went wrong upstream surfaces. There are exactly five major request types, each with distinct workflows depending on whether the policy is direct bill or agency bill.

The Five Service Request Types

1
Certificates of Insurance (COIs)
Proof-of-coverage documents requested by landlords, contractors, clients, and government agencies. A COI summarizes the policy on one page — coverage types, limits, effective dates, certificate holders, and additional insured. Target: issued within 5 minutes. For direct carriers, generate from the carrier portal. For E&S, coordinate with the wholesale broker or issue from Harper's system.
2
Endorsements (Policy Changes)
Any modification to the existing policy: adding/removing locations, changing coverage limits, adding additional insured, updating named insured, or adding coverage types (HNOA, liquor liability, etc.). Direct carriers: self-service or fast turnaround (minutes to hours). E&S carriers: submit through the wholesale broker — expect 3–5 business days. Always confirm the endorsement has been processed before sending an updated COI.
3
Reinstatements
Restoring a policy that has been cancelled or lapsed. Direct carriers typically reinstate quickly if the lapse period is short. E&S reinstatements require the wholesale broker to re-submit to the carrier, which may re-underwrite the risk. Reinstatement is not guaranteed — the carrier can decline. See the Reinstatements section for full process details.
4
Cancellations
Processing policy cancellations — whether initiated by the customer, the carrier (non-payment, underwriting reasons), or the premium finance company. Requires 15+ contact attempts with documented consistency before final processing. Cancellation compliance is strict — see the Cancellations section for rules.
5
Upstream Error Correction
Fixing issues that originated in intake, mid funnel, or sales. Examples: wrong class code on the application, incorrect revenue, missing coverage that should have been included, wrong effective date. Service catches these errors and works backwards through the chain to resolve them — re-quoting, endorsing, or in worst cases, cancelling and rebinding.

06 / REINSTATEMENTS & CANCELLATIONS

Reinstatements & Cancellations

Two of the most operationally complex service requests. The process differs significantly between direct carriers and E&S — and compliance requirements are strict.

Reinstatement Process

Direct Carrier Reinstatement
  • Typically fast — same day if within the grace period
  • Customer pays any outstanding balance
  • Carrier reinstates without re-underwriting in most cases
  • Some carriers allow online reinstatement through the portal
  • If lapse is longer than 30 days, may require a new application
E&S / MGA Reinstatement
  • Submit reinstatement request through the wholesale broker
  • Carrier may re-underwrite the risk — not guaranteed to reinstate
  • May require updated loss runs, financials, or subjectivity documents
  • Timeline: 3–7 business days minimum
  • If the carrier declines reinstatement, must find new coverage (re-quote)
  • Premium finance company must also approve (outstanding balance implications)
Reinstatement Is Not Guaranteed
On E&S policies, the carrier has full discretion to decline reinstatement. A lapse of even a few days can trigger a re-underwriting review. If the customer had claims, late payments, or the risk profile has changed, the carrier may refuse. Always communicate this to the customer immediately — they may need new coverage while the reinstatement is pending.

Cancellation Compliance

Cancellation Contact Requirements
Before a cancellation is finalized, Harper must make a documented good-faith effort to retain the customer or confirm their intent. The standard is:
  • Minimum 15 contact attempts across phone, email, and text
  • Attempts must be consistent and spread over time — 15 calls in one hour does not satisfy the requirement
  • Every attempt must be logged in Big Brother (CRM) with date, time, method, and outcome
  • If the customer responds and confirms cancellation, document the confirmation verbatim
  • If the customer is unreachable after 15+ attempts, proceed with cancellation but keep the full contact log

Cancellation Types

INSURED-INITIATED
Customer Requests Cancellation
Customer contacts Harper to cancel. Confirm the reason, document it, make the 15+ contact attempts to retain, and if confirmed, process. On E&S: 25% MEP applies. On direct bill: minimal cost.
CARRIER-INITIATED
Carrier Cancels for Cause
Carrier issues notice of cancellation — usually for non-payment, missing subjectivities, or underwriting reasons. Harper must contact the customer immediately, attempt resolution, and document all steps.
FINANCE-INITIATED
Premium Finance Cancellation
GoToFinancePremium cancels for non-payment. The finance company notifies the broker and carrier. Harper must contact the customer to resolve the outstanding balance before the cancellation effective date.
Consistency Is the Standard
Regulators and the insurance commissioner evaluate cancellation handling based on consistency — did you follow the same process for every customer? If one customer gets 15 attempts and another gets 3, that's a compliance risk. The process must be the same every time, documented the same way, regardless of the policy size or customer attitude.
07 / SERVICE STANDARDS

Service Mindset & Standards

These are the non-negotiable standards for service operations at Harper. Speed, documentation, and consistency define the customer experience.

The Five Service Rules

1
Speed Wins
COIs and binders should be issued in 5 minutes or less. When a customer or their landlord/contractor asks for proof of coverage, every minute of delay risks losing a lease, a contract, or the customer's trust. Speed is the single biggest differentiator between Harper and every other agency.
2
Same-Day Completion Standard
Every service request received during business hours should be completed the same day. COIs, simple endorsements, reinstatement submissions, cancellation processing — nothing sits overnight unless it requires external action from a carrier or broker that cannot be completed same-day.
3
Big Brother CRM Documentation
Every interaction, every phone call, every email, every status change — logged in Big Brother immediately. This is Harper's advantage. When a customer disputes what happened, when a carrier asks for proof, when a regulator reviews the file — it's all there. Notes must include date, time, method, content summary, and next steps.
4
No Priority Jumping for Demanding Customers
The loudest customer does not go to the front of the line. Every customer gets the same standard of service: same-day completion, same process, same documentation. If a customer calls repeatedly or makes threats, document the interactions and follow the standard process. Consistency protects the team and the company.
5
Escalate System Issues Immediately
If a carrier portal is down, a tool is broken, or a process is failing systemically — escalate immediately. Do not work around the issue silently. A system problem that affects one agent affects all agents. Report it, flag it, and let the team know so it can be resolved for everyone.

Service Mindset Framework

⚡
Speed Over Perfection
A 95% correct COI delivered in 5 minutes beats a 100% perfect one delivered tomorrow. Get it out, then refine if needed.
📋
Document Everything
If it's not in Big Brother, it didn't happen. CRM notes are your best defense in disputes, complaints, and audits.
⚖
Process Is Protection
Following the same process for every customer — regardless of policy size or attitude — is what keeps Harper compliant.
Why Same-Day Matters
A customer waiting for a COI might be signing a lease that afternoon. A contractor waiting for additional insured confirmation might not let the insured on the job site. A reinstatement delayed by a day means the customer is operating without coverage for an extra 24 hours. Every hour of delay has a real-world consequence for the customer's business — and for Harper's retention rate.
Big Brother Is the Advantage
Most agencies rely on email threads and memory. Harper has every call recorded, every note logged, every status change timestamped. When a customer says "nobody told me about the subjectivities," Harper can pull the exact call where it was explained. When a carrier disputes a bind request, Harper has the signed documents and the submission timestamp. This documentation advantage compounds over time — it protects the team, resolves disputes faster, and builds trust with carriers and regulators.
